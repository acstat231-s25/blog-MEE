Methodology

Last updated

April 26, 2025

Data Collection

We constructed a corpus of abortion-related articles from Fox News and The New York Times, covering the period from January 1, 2020 to December 31, 2024, in order to capture discourse both before and after the Supreme Court’s Dobbs v. Jackson Women’s Health Organization decision.

Because of differences in platform structure and data accessibility, we employed different methods: - For The New York Times, articles were gathered by manually crawling search results from The New York Times search portal, filtered for English-language articles related to abortion. - For Fox News, articles were scraped directly from the abortion topic page under the Politics section.

In addition to news articles, we scraped state-level legislative summaries and policy reports from the Guttmacher Institute to contextualize media framing relative to changing abortion laws.

Tools Used for Scraping

Scraping was performed using Python 3.11, with the following libraries:

  • selenium: Allows automated control of a web browser, mimicking human actions like clicking, scrolling, and loading content.
  • beautifulsoup4: Parses the structure of web pages (HTML) and extracts desired information like article text and metadata.
  • requests: Sends direct HTTP requests to static web pages without using a browser.
  • stem (Tor): Routes scraping traffic through multiple anonymous servers to avoid detection or IP blocking.
  • pandas: Organizes scraped data into structured tables (dataframes), allowing easy export to .csv or .json files.

Manual validation was performed to remove duplicates, photo galleries, videos, and non-article content.

Data Preparation

Text preprocessing was conducted using Python libraries specialized for working with text:

  • nltk (Natural Language Toolkit): Tokenizes articles into words and sentences; removes common filler words (stopwords).
  • spacy: Provides more sophisticated linguistic analysis, such as detecting sentence boundaries and named entities.
  • re (regular expressions): Cleans unwanted patterns like multiple spaces, leftover HTML artifacts, or special symbols.

Cleaning steps included: - Converting all text to lowercase - Removing HTML tags, ads, and website navigation text - Retaining critical terms related to abortion debates (e.g., “pro-life”, “choice”, “viability”) to preserve outlet-specific framing

Each article and policy document was assigned a unique ID for traceability.

Analytical Approach

We conducted a multi-stage analysis combining computational and manual techniques:

  • Sentiment Analysis:
    Articles were analyzed for emotional tone using the AFINN and NRC lexicons, implemented through the text2emotion and vaderSentiment libraries.

  • Topic Modeling:
    Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) was applied to both pre-Dobbs and post-Dobbs corpora using gensim, optimized for coherence to ensure meaningful topic clusters.

  • Framing Analysis:
    A stratified random sample of articles was manually coded to classify dominant frames, following typologies adapted from Entman (1993) and Gamson & Modigliani (1989), including legalistic, rights-based, moral/religious, and public health frames.

All computational analysis was conducted in reproducible environments managed by conda, and all code was version-controlled with git.

Comparative Analysis

We systematically compared Fox News and The New York Times coverage along three key dimensions: 1. Sentiment Shifts: Changes in emotional tone over time and across outlets. 2. Topic Evolution: Emerging and declining themes before and after Dobbs. 3. Framing Strategies: Variations in how abortion was framed as legal, moral, political, or medical.

Visualizations were produced using matplotlib and seaborn.
